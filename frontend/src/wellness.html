Physical Health Tips

Take care of your body:

  • Stay active: Engage in regular exercise (walking, yoga, etc.).
  • Eat well-balanced meals with plenty of fruits and vegetables.
  • Get enough restful sleep each night.
  • Stay hydrated.

Water Intake

Proper hydration is essential for overall health. Here's what you need to know:

  • Recommended Daily Intake: Aim for about 8 cups (64 ounces) of water per day.
  • Adjust Based on Needs: Adjust your water intake based on activity level, climate, and individual requirements.
  • Hydration Tips:
    • Carry a reusable water bottle with you.
    • Set reminders to drink water throughout the day.
    • Incorporate hydrating foods like fruits and vegetables into your diet.

Self-Care Routines

Prioritize self-care:

  • Practice skincare rituals (cleansing, moisturizing).
  • Set aside time for relaxation (meditation, deep breathing).
  • Engage in hobbies you enjoy (reading, painting, gardening).

Fertility Checks

Definition:
Fertility checks are medical tests used to evaluate a person's ability to conceive.
Types of tests:
  • Ovulation testing (for women)
  • Hormone level testing (for both men and women)

    • What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will perform a pelvic exam and blood work (for women) to test hormone levels and ovulation.
    Why it's important:
    Fertility checks can help identify potential issues and provide guidance for conceiving.

    Pap Smear Tests

    Definition:
    Pap smear tests are medical tests used to screen for cervical cancer.
    What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will collect a sample of cells from the cervix to examine for abnormal cell changes.
    Why it's important:
    Pap smear tests can help detect cervical cancer early on, when it's most treatable.

    Breast Cancer Screening

    Definition:
    Breast cancer screening is a medical test used to detect breast cancer.
    Types of tests:
  • Mammogram (X-ray imaging)
  • Clinical breast exam (physical exam by a healthcare provider)

    • What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will perform a mammogram or clinical breast exam to detect any abnormalities.
    Why it's important:
    Breast cancer screening can help detect cancer early on, when it's most treatable.

    STI Checks

    Definition:
    STI checks are medical tests used to detect sexually transmitted infections. Types of tests:
  • Blood tests
  • Urine tests
  • wab tests (for genital, oral, or rectal infections)

    • What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will collect a blood or urine sample or perform a swab test to detect STIs. Why it's important:
    STI checks can help identify and treat infections early on, preventing long-term damage and transmission to others.

    Blood Pressure Checks

    Definition:
    Blood pressure checks are medical tests used to measure blood pressure.
    What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will use a blood pressure cuff to measure blood pressure.
    Why it's important:
    Blood pressure checks can help identify high blood pressure, which can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.

    Cholesterol Checks

    Definition:
    Cholesterol checks are medical tests used to measure cholesterol levels.
    What to expect:
    A healthcare provider will collect a blood sample to measure cholesterol levels.
    Why it's important:
    Cholesterol checks can help identify high cholesterol, which can increase the risk of heart disease and stroke.
